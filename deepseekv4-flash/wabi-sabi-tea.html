The beauty of simplicity and austerity. A single flower in a rough clay vase. The quiet elegance of untouched wood. Finding richness in restraint.

Sabi

The beauty of age and wear. A bronze teapot, softened by decades of use. Cracks filled with gold — kintsugi — making brokenness the most precious part.

Cha

The way of tea. Not a beverage, but a practice — a ritual of presence. Each bowl prepared with complete attention. Each sip, a meditation on impermanence.
